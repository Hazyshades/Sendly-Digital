# Reclaim Contracts

Short description: the documents below record what was added to work with the Reclaim Protocol in Arc Testnet and why it is needed. The files in this folder are the minimal on-chain set for verifying zkTLS proofs without QR/mobile apps/browser extensions at the contract layer. The source of the contracts is the official Reclaim SDK (`reclaimprotocol/reclaim-solidity-sdk`).

## What was created and why

### `Reclaim.sol`
The Reclaim Protocol verifier contract for on-chain proof verification. This is the official SDK contract with no custom logic.

Why it is needed:
- Confirms that the proof is signed by Reclaim witnesses and matches the active epoch.
- Validates the proof structure at the `ClaimInfo` → `identifier` level.
- Used by `ZkSend.sol` as an external verifier contract.

Key parts:
- **Epoch**: a set of witnesses and a minimum signature threshold.
- **addNewEpoch**: adds a new epoch (performed by the owner).
- **verifyProof**: verifies the proof and reverts on error.
- **epochDurationS**: epoch duration (used to select witnesses).
- **semaphoreAddress**: address of the Semaphore contract, required to verify Merkle identity.

Deployment notes:
- The contract is upgradeable (UUPS) and uses `initialize(...)` instead of a constructor.
- A valid `semaphoreAddress` is required, otherwise some proof/identity functions will not work.

### `lib/*`
Official SDK libraries referenced by `Reclaim.sol`:
- `Claims.sol`, `StringUtils.sol`, `BytesUtils.sol`, `Random.sol`
- `SemaphoreInterface.sol` (interface for the Semaphore contract)

Minimum requirements:
- At least one epoch must be configured via `addNewEpoch`.
- Witnesses must be real Reclaim addresses (otherwise valid proofs will fail).

### `ZkSend.sol`
Direct payments contract to a social identity (no NFT), using Reclaim proofs.

Why it is needed:
- Allows sending tokens to `socialIdentityHash` and letting only the account owner confirmed by a proof claim them.
- Avoids NFT minting and vault contracts (simplifies the logic).

What was updated:
- Added `nonReentrant` protection on `claimPayment`.
- Validates that `recipient` matches `proof.signedClaim.claim.owner`.
- Compares `context` from `claimInfo` with `socialIdentityHash`:
  - extracts `contextMessage` from `context` (if present),
  - then computes `keccak256("platform:username")` and compares it to `socialIdentityHash`.

## Why this satisfies the “no QR/mobile/extensions” requirement
Contracts accept proofs as normal data and do not depend on how they are obtained.  
That means QR/mobile app/extension restrictions apply only to the **proof generation process**, not to on-chain verification.

## How it is used (flow logic)
1. The user obtains a proof (any acceptable off-chain method).
2. `ZkSend.createPayment(...)` creates a payment to `socialIdentityHash`.
3. `ZkSend.claimPayment(...)` accepts the proof and recipient address.
4. `ZkSend` calls `Reclaim.verifyProof(...)` and checks the identity hash.

## Important notes
- Without configured epochs and witnesses in `Reclaim.sol`, verification will fail.
- `socialIdentityHash` is `keccak256("platform:username")` in lowercase, otherwise mismatches are possible.
- Real Reclaim witness addresses are required for production use.
